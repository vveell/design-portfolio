To analyze what makes a design good, I found an album cover that I appreciated. In InDesign, I created a graphic that highlighted certain aspects of the chosen design.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

In the logo design project, we utilized premade templates from Canva and Adobe. This brings up the ethical question on original vs existing content. While using technology in the creative process, the convenient tools online can push the boundaries of creating something from the individual. Additionally, rhetoric in the context of design was used in the UX testing project, where interviewee’s perceptions of the 3 logos was noted.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Specifically in the Good Design project, understanding design principles was necessary to analyze the chosen example of graphic design. In the logo project, alignment and proximity were what I focused on to best create appealing logos. I especially worked to make sure my text and other elements met these principles to appear coordinated.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment

In our small group time, I enjoyed understanding how I could improve by seeing other’s works. It was greatly revealing to see how my peers approached each project, as the design process was reflective of themselves. I enjoyed seeing everyone’s chosen picture in the cartoon project, as it reflected not only how they see themselves but how they stylized their portraits.



4. Practice revision and across media forms

Revision was an integral part in the UX Testing project. I best revised what information I gathered on my interviewee’s insights. After incorporating feedback I improved my final design greatly. Also, in analyzing my classmates' work, I could generate advancements to my own projects.

5. Learn to present your work.

While I am still very shy, I got a little better in my presentation skills. Especially in the Cartoon project, I was very nervous to show my classmates because I wanted to do well, but I think that is more of a me thing. I now know what I need to do in order to better my communication and presenting skills.

Graphic Design Resume

Creating a resume in InDesign after reviewing models of online resumes, and important aspects of resume effectiveness.

Advertisement

An ad for perfume with pastel colors.

Client Project

2 different logos for Jelgatos Camera, for an independent photographer. Created with InDesign and photo editor tools.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Specifically in my client project, I was asked to include a famous meme that could not be used on a more professional scale. However, I did notice a plethora of images being used as merchandise to be sold online. Ethical considerations were on fair use, and again the use of online resources.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

In the short term projects I used the design principle of kerning/spacing the most. In all four projects, the spacing was altered to best suit its purpose. For example, in the resume, I changed the kerning on my bigger pieces of text to make it easier to read. In my Ad and 2 Page Spread, I focused on balance and consistency to create a clear theme.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

I received great feedback on my 2 Page spread project in comparison to my source, and more insight on what details I missed. For the Advertisement project, I gathered inspiration in group discussions on which direction to go with my ad.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Revision was essential for improving my projects in the short term. Many adjustments were necessary for the layout of my advertisement and resume. In my final client meeting with A and B samples, I was asked to change elements of the logo to my client’s wishes.

5. Learn to present your work.

The short term projects felt more difficult to showcase as they were more in relation to my skills. Especially with a clear purpose in an advertisement, I had to be more persuasive when presenting. As I have more experience presenting, I feel like my communication is more confident.
